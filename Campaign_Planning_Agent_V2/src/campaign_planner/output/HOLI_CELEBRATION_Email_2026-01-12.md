Holi—the vibrant Festival of Colors—is a joyful celebration observed by millions around the world. As we build a workplace where everyone’s traditions are seen and respected, we’re sharing quick facts and practical ways to acknowledge Holi thoughtfully with colleagues.

Did you know?
- Holi marks the arrival of spring and the triumph of good over adversity.
- Celebrations often include Holika Dahan (a bonfire night) followed by Rangwali Holi, the colorful day of play.
- Colors symbolize joy, unity, and renewal—many greet with “Happy Holi!” and share sweets and music.
- Traditions vary by region and community, so observance looks different for each person and family.

Inclusive ways to acknowledge Holi at work:
- Offer warm greetings (“Happy Holi!”) and show curiosity with respectful questions.
- Be mindful with colors—always get consent, avoid scented powders, and respect personal boundaries.
- Consider dietary needs and fasting practices; provide inclusive, labeled snacks if celebrating.
- Share learning resources and give colleagues space to observe in their own way.
